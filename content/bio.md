I am Jinze Li, an undergraduate student in Economics at Nanjing University.


My research interests lie at the intersection of:

- Urban Economics
- Regional Development
- Spatial Econometrics
- Artificial Intelligence and Geospatial Data


I develop computational approaches combining economic theory,
causal inference, machine learning, and remote sensing to study
urban transformation and regional inequality.



My current research projects include:

**The Location Curse: FUA Integration and Industrial Hollowing in China**

Investigating how metropolitan integration reshapes industrial development
in surrounding regions using causal inference and spatial econometric methods.



**AI-driven Urban Renewal Monitoring System**

Building a multi-source remote sensing framework integrating
Landsat, VIIRS, POI, OSM and GHSL data for urban renewal assessment.



**China Real Estate Policy Database**

Developing an open research infrastructure for structured Chinese
real estate policy analysis.



I am interested in computational economics,
spatial intelligence, and data-driven urban studies.
